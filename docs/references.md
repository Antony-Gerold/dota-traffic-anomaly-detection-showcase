# References

Cited in the final report:

1. **Y. Yao, X. Wang, M. Xu, Z. Pu, Y. Wang, E. Atkins, D. J. Crandall** (2023). *DoTA: Unsupervised Detection of Traffic Anomaly in Driving Videos.* IEEE TPAMI. — The benchmark dataset and the FOL-Ensemble baseline this work compares against.
2. **W. Bao, Q. Yu, Y. Kong** (2020). *Uncertainty-based Traffic Accident Anticipation with Spatio-Temporal Relational Learning.* ACM MM. — CCD dataset.
3. **Y. Zhang, P. Sun, Y. Jiang, et al.** (2022). *ByteTrack: Multi-Object Tracking by Associating Every Detection Box.* ECCV. — The tracking algorithm; the two-stage low-confidence re-association is what makes it suitable for anomalous-object tracking.
4. **G. Jocher et al.** (2023). *Ultralytics YOLOv8.* — Detector backbone.
5. **M. Oquab, T. Darcet, T. Moutakanni, et al.** (2023). *DINOv2: Learning Robust Visual Features without Supervision.* Meta AI. — Frozen visual backbone used for Path B fusion.
6. **G. Ke, Q. Meng, T. Finley, et al.** (2017). *LightGBM: A Highly Efficient Gradient Boosting Decision Tree.* NeurIPS. — Classifier A and Stage-2 backbone.
7. **A. Bewley, Z. Ge, L. Ott, F. Ramos, B. Upcroft** (2016). *Simple Online and Realtime Tracking (SORT).* ICIP. — Baseline compared against ByteTrack.
8. **N. Wojke, A. Bewley, D. Paulus** (2017). *Simple Online and Realtime Tracking with a Deep Association Metric (DeepSORT).* ICIP. — Discussed in the rejected-alternatives section.
9. **T. Fernando, S. Denman, S. Sridharan, C. Fookes** (2022). *Deep Learning for Medical Anomaly Detection: A Survey.* ACM Comp. Surveys. — Anomaly-detection methodology background.
